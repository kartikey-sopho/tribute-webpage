Steve Jobs

“Don’t let the noise of others’ opinions drown out your own inner voice."

Here's a timeline of Jobs Life:

  • 1955: Born in San Francisco, USA
  • 1972: Enroll in Reeds College, Oregon but drops out after a semester
  • 1974: Works for Atari and attends meetings with Steve Wozniak
  • 1976: Apple is founded on april fool's day. Apple 1 goes on sale
  • 1978: Job's daughter Lisa is born to his girlfriend Chrisann Brennan
  • 1980: Apple goes public raising $110 million
  • 1985: Clash with John Sculley, resigns from CEO. Steve Wozniak also leaves apple
  • 1986: Starts Next Inc. making high end machines for universities, also buys Pixar
  • 1989: First NeXT computer goes on sale
  • 1996: Apple buys Next for $430 million for the operating system Jobs' team developed, Jobs appointed as advisor to the CEO
  • 1997: Becomes 'interim' CEO, puts an end to Mac clones
  • 2000: 'interim' label removed from the post as Apple returns to profitablility
  • 2001: First 'iPod' goes on sale, as do computers with OS X, the new operating system, iTunes software is also released.
  • 2004: Undergoes surgery for a rare but cureable form of pancreatic cancer
  • 2006: Disney buys Pixar for $7.4 billion. Steve Jobs becomes Disney's largest single shareholder
  • 2007: Apple releases its first smartphone, iPhone
  • Jan 17, 2011: In a memo to Apple employees, Jobs announced a second medical leave with no set duration. Tim Cook steps in to run day-to-day operations while Jobs still retains CEO title.
  • Aug. 24, 2011: Jobs resigns as CEO and named as chairman, Tim Cook becomes the CEO.
  • Oct. 5, 2011: Jobs dies at the age of 56, Apple announces his death without giving any reason
